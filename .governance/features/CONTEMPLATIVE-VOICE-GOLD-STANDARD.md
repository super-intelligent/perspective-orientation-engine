## THE CLAIM

"My co-founder keeps overriding product decisions after we agree on them."

## THE FULL CONTEMPLATIVE EXPLANATION

This claim sits in the upper-right quadrant of the four Wilber quadrants,
which is the exterior individual domain. That means we are dealing with
something observable in behavior, not an internal feeling or a shared
cultural meaning. The key signal here is that this is something you could
point to and document. Decisions are made, and then they are overridden.
That makes it behavioral, not interpretive.

The domain is Complex, which means cause-and-effect relationships are not
stable or predictable in advance. Even though the behavior is visible,
the underlying pattern is not reducible to a single clear cause. The
repeated overriding suggests an emergent relational dynamic that only
becomes visible over time, rather than something that can be solved
through a single intervention.

The reasoning mode is Abductive, which means this claim is constructed
as the best available explanation given incomplete information. You are
not just observing that decisions are being overridden. You are implicitly
constructing a model of what that behavior means about trust, control,
or alignment, even though you cannot fully verify the internal state
driving it. The abductive element is the bridge between what is seen
and what is inferred.

Relevance is High, because this pattern directly affects execution,
trust, and decision integrity. It is not background context. It is
actively shaping how you interpret the viability of the partnership
and the system you are building together.

The archetype label for this claim is Decision Authority Instability.
What this compresses is not just that decisions are being overridden,
but that the structure of authority itself is not holding. The system
of agreement is not stable, and that instability is being expressed
through repeated behavioral overrides.

In relation to a potential orientation center, this claim would likely
act as structural support for a broader realization about breakdown in
co-founder alignment. It provides concrete behavioral evidence that the
agreement structure is not functioning as intended, which feeds into a
larger pattern of systemic instability in the partnership.

## VOICE RULES (Derived from this example)

- Explain how the claim BEHAVES in the system, not what it means
- No evaluation, no advice, no correction
- Only structural explanation
- Name axes in full English, not abbreviations
- Define each axis, then show the specific instantiation
- The secret ingredient is always SPECIFICITY OF INSTANTIATION:
  what is specifically [this mode] about THIS claim
