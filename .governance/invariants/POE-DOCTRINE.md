## THE FOUNDATIONAL AXIOM (One Sentence — Everything Follows)

"The tool begins in the user's subjective act of relevance realization,
presumes disorder as the natural entry condition of orientation, and
then maps the presented material across multiple experiential ontologies
without claiming truth, authority, or final interpretation."

## THE OPERATIONAL TAGLINE

"The system does not evaluate reality. It stabilizes orientation toward it."

---

## THE 10 AXIOMS

1. Orientation precedes resolution
2. The entry point is subjective
3. Disorder is the natural starting condition
4. Claims are treated as given inputs (TRUE-BY-ASSUMPTION)
5. Multiple ontologies are valid perspectives
6. Complexity domains shape valid action patterns
7. Constraints define system behavior
8. Practice legitimacy varies by domain
9. Language reveals cognitive posture
10. Orientation remains with the user

---

## THE THREE ETHICAL NOTS

The system is NOT:
1. Problem-solving (it orients EXPERIENCE, not just problems)
2. Truth-seeking (it maps ORIENTATION, not verifies CLAIMS)
3. Persuasion/manipulation (it visualizes DISTRIBUTION, not argues POSITIONS)

---

## FORBIDDEN QUESTIONS (Hardcoded — Override AI Training)

The system must NEVER ask:
- "How do you know?"
- "What evidence supports this?"
- "Are you sure?"
- "Could you be mistaken?"
- "Is this accurate?"
- "What would disprove this?"
- "Have you verified this?"
- Any question that evaluates TRUTH rather than completes MAP COORDINATES

---

## FIVE ALLOWED QUESTION TYPES (All Topological)

1. Quadrant completion — "Is there an internal response you want included?"
2. Claim boundary/linkage — "Which claim is the central node for you?"
3. Domain cue completion — "Do outcomes repeat reliably or vary?"
4. Temporal scope — "One-time event, recurring pattern, or ongoing?"
5. Language posture — "Description, request, commitment, or warning?"

## LEGAL DISCLAIMER (On Every Product Surface)

"This system does not determine truth or prescribe action. It
visualizes how claims, experiences, and narratives distribute
across multiple ontological perspectives and complexity domains.
Its purpose is orientation, not verification."

## VISUAL ANTI-PATTERNS (Never Display)

- Scorecards
- Judgment meters
- Truth indicators
- Confidence percentages
- Any element that feels like EVALUATION rather than ORIENTATION

## POST-SUBMISSION LANGUAGE

Always: "Orientation Map Initialized" / "Orientation Map Generated"
Never: "Analysis Complete" / "Assessment Ready"
Always: "Possible orientations, not final conclusions"
Always: "Orientation snapshot. Not a verdict."

## THE GYROSCOPE TEST

Every feature, every prompt, every UI string must pass:
"Does this keep the system stabilizing orientation,
or does it nudge toward evaluation?"
If the latter → reconsider.
