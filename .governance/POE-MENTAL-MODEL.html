field_coherence vs distribution_profile — The Critical Distinction

These are orthogonal. A field can have strong coherence (clear gravitational center) AND distributed profile (claims spread across all quadrants with active tensions). The center emerged from a broad-basis field. OR it can have strong coherence AND concentrated profile — the center emerged from a narrow-basis field. Neither is better or worse. They are structurally different.

competing bifurcated Two attractors, each drawing from a distinct quadrant cluster.
distributed distributed Wide open field. No center, high coverage, high tension density.

The Landauer Connection

Each stage of the lifecycle corresponds to how much possibility space has been foreclosed. Drift = beginning of premature collapse. Recovery = possibility space reopening. The distribution_profile is the observable proxy for this.

The POE was already designed as an engine of sustained possibility (TRUE-BY-ASSUMPTION, deferred commitment). Landauer provides the structural logic for WHY that design is correct: premature collapse is expensive, and the cost shows up as orientation failure.

What the System Detects (No Evaluation)

The system never says "you are drifting" or "you have collapsed." It shows the distribution shape. The user interprets what it means for their situation.

A concentrated field is structurally described: "Claims distribute across one quadrant. Low tension density." Whether that concentration is appropriate for the situation — the user decides. Some situations genuinely live in one quadrant.

The Divergence Map — What Enterprise Sees

Four people orient from LR/Complex (institutional structure, emergent). One person — Person E — orients from UL/Complex (interior individual, emergent). That single divergent orientation is likely the most valuable signal in the room.

The system surfaces it without declaring it correct or incorrect. "One observer orients this situation primarily from the interior individual perspective. Four orient from the exterior collective perspective." The team decides what to do with that.

Convergent Claims

Claims that appear across multiple observer maps. High probability of structural signal.

Unique Perspectives

Claims that appear in only one map. Possible blind spots for the majority — or important minority knowledge.

Domain Disagreements

Same situation content classified in different Cynefin domains. This is the most actionable signal — it reveals where the team is applying different reasoning models.

Layer 08 — Constitutional

The Doctrine — What Never Changes

The Three-Call Architecture Enacts the Doctrine

Call 1 is blind to Call 2

The extraction model never knows it will be observed. This is the same logic as TRUE-BY-ASSUMPTION — the system does not pre-judge. Each call holds only the context it needs, nothing more.

Call 2 reads structure, not content

The field analysis model reads JSON metrics — counts and ratios — not claims. This is the closest the system gets to pure objectivity. Arithmetic does not evaluate truth.

Call 3 receives only ambiguity

In Phase 2, the probe-generation model receives only the list of low-signal placements — not the full brain dump. Narrow context produces clean probe questions.
